Three things we need confirmed before either page goes live

Each of these affects copy that is already on the pages. Nothing ships until they are answered.

1. What exactly does the 90-day guarantee cover on a multi-month order? The returns page states the guarantee “applies only to the first shipment of your 3-month subscription.” If a customer buys the $238 three-month option and returns it on day 80, is the refund $238 or one month’s portion? Both pages lean on the guarantee as the primary risk-reversal, so we need the precise answer before publishing it.
2. Is the clinical figure 682% or 628%? Packaging, the DTC site and every retail listing read 682%. Page 10 of the 2025 brand book reads 628%. We have used 682% in the locked clinical wording throughout — please confirm in writing.
3. Can we verify the testimonials against the live review platform? The quotes currently on the pages were transcribed from the brand book’s site mockups. Before publishing we want to match them to live, attributable reviews so every quote is verifiable.
4. Can we get the brand photography library? The lifestyle photography on these drafts is placeholder imagery we generated to the brand book’s art direction (cinematic, crushed blacks, directional light). Product shots are pulled from the live Shopify CDN. The 2025 brand book calls for cinematic and black-and-white athlete photography, which would lift both pages considerably — we would like access to those source files rather than approximating them.
Also worth a look: Two figures published on the current site are inconsistent with each other — the patent count (stated as “15 U.S. patents,” while the footnote lists 14 U.S. plus 1 Canadian) and the units-sold number, which appears as three different figures on three different pages. We have avoided both on these drafts.

How to review these

Open on a phone

Both pages are built mobile-first and the majority of traffic will land there. Desktop is the secondary view.

Read the whole scroll

Each page is built as one argument from top to bottom. Judging a section in isolation will read differently than in sequence.

Flag claims, not taste

Most useful feedback: anything that overstates a claim or conflicts with approved language. Design notes welcome after that.
